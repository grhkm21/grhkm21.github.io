Bernoulli Polynomials

Outlining what can be done on bernoulli polynomials in Lean

Jan 25, 2023
5 minute read

Please note that $p$ does not denote a prime, but instead usually an integer $p \geq 0$.

Existing lemmas

Definition by recurrence:

$$ B_n = 1 - \sum_{k = 0}^{n - 1} \binom{n}{k} \frac{B_k}{n + 1 - k} $$

$$ B_n(x) = \sum_{k = 0}^n \binom{n}{k} B_{n - k}x^k $$

Faulhaber’s theorem:

$$ \sum_{k = 0}^{n - 1} k^p = \sum_{i = 0}^p B_i \binom{p + 1}{i} \frac{n^{p + 1 - i}}{p + 1} $$

Which has been formalised in Lean:

theorem sum_range_pow (n p : ℕ) :
  ∑ k in range n, (k : ℚ) ^ p =
    ∑ i in range (p + 1), bernoulli i * (p + 1).choose i * n ^ (p + 1 - i) / (p + 1) :=

Exponential Generating Function of Bernoulli numbers:

$$ \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \frac{B_k}{k!} t^k = \frac{t}{e^t - 1} $$

Which has been formalised in Lean:

def bernoulli_power_series := mk $ λ n, algebra_map ℚ A (bernoulli n / n!)

theorem bernoulli_power_series_mul_exp_sub_one :
  bernoulli_power_series A * (exp A - 1) = X :=

We also have the following result:

$$ B_n(0) = (-1)^n B_n(1) $$

Which has been formalised in Lean:

@[simp] lemma bernoulli_eval_zero (n : ℕ) : (bernoulli n).eval 0 = _root_.bernoulli n :=
@[simp] lemma bernoulli_eval_one (n : ℕ) : (bernoulli n).eval 1 = _root_.bernoulli' n :=
def bernoulli (n : ℕ) : ℚ := (-1)^n * bernoulli' n

Lemmas

Below are lemmas that can to be proven:

Lemma 1 (Exponential Generating Function of Bernoulli polynomials):

$$ \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \frac{B_k(x)}{k!} t^k = \frac{te^{tx}}{e^t - 1} $$

Proof from proofwiki:

As usual, we have $e^{tx} = \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \frac{x^k}{k!} t^k$, also formalised as

def exp : power_series A := mk $ λ n, algebra_map ℚ A (1 / n!)

Therefore, we multiply to get

\begin{align*} \frac{te^{tx}}{e^t - 1} &= \left(\sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \frac{B_k}{k!} t^k\right)\left(\sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \frac{x^k}{k!} t^k\right) \\ &= \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} t^k \sum_{m = 0}^k \frac{B_{k - m}}{(k - m)!} \frac{x^m}{m!} \\ &= \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \frac{t^k}{k!} \sum_{m = 0}^k \frac{k!}{(k - m)!m!} B_{k - m} x^m \\ &= \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \frac{t^k}{k!} \sum_{m = 0}^k \binom{k}{m} B_{k - m} x^m \\ &= \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \frac{B_k(x)}{k!} t^k \end{align*}

It might be easier to just prove that each coefficient of $t^k$ is equal, since that’s essentially what we are doing.

Lemma 2 (Faulhaber’s Theorem in terms of bernoulli polynomials): For integers $n \geq 1$ and $p \geq 0$,

$$ \sum_{k = 0}^{n - 1} k^p = \frac{B_{p + 1}(n) - B_{p + 1}}{p + 1} $$

Proof:

\begin{align*} \frac{B_{p + 1}(n) - B_{p + 1}}{p + 1} &= \frac{1}{p + 1}\left(\sum_{k = 0}^{p + 1} \binom{p + 1}{k} B_{p + 1 - k} n^k - B_{p + 1}\right) \\ &= \frac{1}{p + 1}\left(\sum_{k = 1}^{p + 1} \binom{p + 1}{k} B_{p + 1 - k} n^k\right) \\ &= \frac{1}{p + 1}\left(\sum_{k = 0}^p \binom{p + 1}{k + 1} B_{p - k} n^{k + 1}\right) \\ &= \frac{1}{p + 1}\left(\sum_{k = 0}^p \binom{p + 1}{(p - k) + 1} B_k n^{(p - k) + 1}k\right) & k \mapsto p - k \\ &= \frac{1}{p + 1}\left(\sum_{k = 0}^p \binom{p + 1}{k} B_k n^{p + 1 - k}\right) \\ &= \sum_{i = 0}^p B_i \binom{p + 1}{i} \frac{n^{p + 1 - i}}{p + 1} \\ \end{align*}

Lemma 3 (Forward difference of bernoulli polynomials): For integers $n \geq 0$ and $p \geq 1$,

$$ B_p(n + 1) - B_p(n) = pn^{p - 1} $$

Proof: Actually, this is equivalent to lemma 1! Rewriting lemma 1, we have

$$ B_p(n) = p\left(\sum_{k = 0}^{n - 1} k^{p - 1}\right) + B_p $$

\begin{align*} B_p(n + 1) - B_p(n) &= \left(p\left(\sum_{k = 0}^n k^{p - 1}\right) + B_p\right) \\ &- \left(p\left(\sum_{k = 0}^{n - 1} k^{p - 1}\right) + B_p\right) \\ &= pn^{p - 1} \end{align*}

 This identity holds for $n < 0$ too. In fact, it holds for any $n \in \mathbb{R}$. Fix it.

Lemma 4 (Derivative of Bernoulli polynomials): For integers $n \geq 1$,

$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} B_n(x) = nB_{n - 1}(x) $$

Proof: From the basic identity $\binom{n}{k + 1} \cdot (k + 1) = \frac{n!}{(n - k - 1)!(k + 1)!} = \frac{(n - 1)!}{(n - k - 1)!k!} \cdot n$, we get that

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} B_n(x) &= \sum_{k = 1}^n \binom{n}{k} B_{n - k} \left(kx^{k - 1}\right) \\ &= \sum_{k = 0}^{n - 1} \binom{n}{k + 1} B_{n - 1 - k} \left((k + 1)x^k\right) \\ &= n\sum_{k = 0}^{n - 1} \binom{n - 1}{k} B_{n - 1 - k} x^k \\ &= nB_{n - 1}(x) \end{align*}

Theorems

Theorem (Symmetry): For integers $n \geq 0$,

$$ B_n(x) = (-1)^n B_n(1 - x) $$

Proof: Define the polynomial $E_n(x) = B_n(1 - x) - (-1)^n B_n(x)$. It suffices to show that $E_n(x) = 0$. We perform induction on $n$. For $n = 0$, it is so trivial that simp can probably do it. Assume it is true for $n = k$, then

\begin{align*} \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} E_{k + 1}(x) &= -(k + 1)B_k(1 - x) - (-1)^{k + 1} (k + 1)B_k(x) \\ &= -(k + 1)\left(B_k(1 - x) - (-1)^k B_k(x)\right) \\ &= -(k + 1)E_k(x) \\ &= 0 \end{align*}

This means that $E_k(x)$ is constant. Finally, we know that $E_k(0) = B_n(1) - (-1)^n B_n(0) = 0$, finishing the proof.
